    Quantum Computing
    Final Report

    In Summer 2021, I spent time with Professor Peter Schupp and another undergraduate student on research about Quantum Computing. Quantum Computing is an emergent technology that uses quantum parallelism, entanglement, and other quantum effects to speed up computations and allow tackling problems that would otherwise forever be out of reach for even the fastest supercomputers. If successful, the implications would be truly revolutionary, but many technical hurdles remain.
    In my internship on Quantum Computing, we did research on state of the art in hardware, algorithms, applications, and quantum computing programming languages. Besides these, our main focus was to understand the today's technical difficulties in developing a scalable quantum computer. Different models such as using superconducting qubits (implemented by Google), ion-trapped method, photonic quantum computation, semiconductor qubits, and topological qubits were our primary focus of research. Their technical implementations and difficulties in developing a scalable quantum computer were researched. Few known quantum algorithms were studied and implemented in IBM's quantum computer. The major part of the internship was focused on study of quantum cellular automata, which is another model for quantum computation. Various models and their realization to simulate a quantum circuit or quantum Turing machine was the primary focus of the internship.
    Alike to any research, documentation of research, slides preparation, and presentations were subroutines tasks that were carried out perfectly.
